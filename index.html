Benchmarking AI in Scientific Literature Reviews

A collaborative research study evaluating the effectiveness of AI-assisted workflows for conducting scientific literature reviews

Led by researchers from the University of Toronto, Department of Psychology
Contribute Your Research Learn More

About the Study

Our Mission

We are conducting a systematic evaluation of AI and AI-assisted workflows for scientific literature reviews. By comparing AI-only and human-AI collaborative approaches against expert-led research, we aim to establish benchmarks for AI performance in academic research.

Why This Matters

As AI tools become increasingly prevalent in research, understanding their capabilities and limitations in literature review tasks is crucial for the scientific community. This study will provide empirical evidence about the effectiveness of AI assistance in research workflows.

Your Contribution

We are seeking unpublished or near-complete literature reviews from active researchers in experimental psychology, neuroscience, and related fields. Your work will serve as the gold standard for evaluating AI performance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How will my research be used?
Your literature review will serve as a gold standard benchmark. We will use it to evaluate how well AI-assisted methods can identify key papers, themes, and arguments in your field. Your work will be handled with complete confidentiality and will not be shared publicly.
What recognition will I receive?
Contributors will be offered authorship on the resulting publication. Your expertise and contribution will be formally acknowledged, and the publication will bring additional visibility to your research area.
Can I participate if my paper is under review?
Yes! We accept literature reviews at any stage before publication, including those under review. We only require that the work has not been publicly published yet.
How much time commitment is required?
Minimal! We only need you to provide: (1) a brief summary of your research question, (2) an outline of key themes/arguments, and (3) a list of critical citations or bibliography. This typically takes 15-30 minutes.
Who can participate in the hackathon?
The hackathon will be open to graduate students, postdocs, and researchers. Details about registration and eligibility will be announced after we complete the literature review collection phase.
